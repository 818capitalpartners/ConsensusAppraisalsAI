## How Fix & Flip Loans Work

Fix and flip loans (also called bridge loans or rehab loans) are short-term financing designed for investors who buy, renovate, and sell properties for profit. Unlike traditional mortgages, these loans are:

- **Short-term:** 12-24 month terms
- **Interest-only:** Lower monthly payments during the rehab period
- **Based on the deal:** Lenders focus on the property's After Repair Value (ARV), not just your income
- **Fast closing:** Many lenders can close in 5-10 business days

## Key Metrics Lenders Evaluate

### Loan-to-Cost (LTC)

**LTC = Loan Amount / Total Project Cost**

Total project cost includes purchase price + rehab budget. Most lenders fund up to 85-90% LTC, meaning you bring 10-15% of total costs to the table.

### After Repair Value (ARV)

The estimated value of the property after renovations. Lenders typically cap at 70-75% of ARV.

### Profit Margin

Smart investors target a minimum 15-20% profit margin after all costs (purchase, rehab, holding costs, selling costs, loan costs).

## Typical Fix & Flip Loan Terms

- **LTC:** Up to 90% (some lenders go to 92.5%)
- **Rehab Funding:** Up to 100% of rehab costs
- **Term:** 12-24 months
- **Rates:** 9-13% (varies by experience and deal quality)
- **Points:** 1.5-3 origination points
- **Draw Schedule:** Rehab funds released in draws as work is completed
- **Experience:** Some lenders require 1-3 completed projects

## Tips for Getting Funded

1. **Have a detailed scope of work** — itemized rehab budget with contractor bids
2. **Know your comps** — provide 3-5 comparable sales supporting your ARV
3. **Show experience** — a track record of completed flips gets better terms
4. **Have reserves** — 3-6 months of interest payments in reserves
5. **Work with a broker** — access to multiple lenders means competitive terms
